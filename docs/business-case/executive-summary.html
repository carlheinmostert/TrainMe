What each scenario actually describes

Three plausible stories, same cost curve.

Same infra, same VAT treatment, same PayFast fees. Only the assumptions about how fast the thing grows differ.

Conservative

Side project. Good for Carl and friends.

Roughly 13 practitioners paying at Year 5, drawn from Melissa's warm network and a handful of conference talks. Revenue barely covers infra and a modest marketing budget; founder stays on zero salary. This is the "we shipped something good but it didn't catch fire" picture.

Base

Solo-founder bootstrap, near break-even.

~175 paying practitioners by Year 5, a paid mix that blends LinkedIn + Google + a real referral loop. Founder takes R25k/mo from Year 1, adds a CS hire in Year 3 and an engineer in Year 4. Five-year cumulative profit lands just on the positive side of zero — validates the lane without needing a raise.

Optimistic

Word-of-mouth wins. International by Year 2.

~1,100 paying practitioners, 8× the Base case. Lower churn (SaaS healthcare-lock territory), more plans per practitioner per month, and international expansion starting in Year 2. Looks like a fundable / sellable SaaS at Year 5 scale — meaningful profit margin, real team.

For the full mechanics, open the xlsx — every driver is editable, every downstream number recomputes. Numbers baked from the 2026-04-20 xlsx baseline. Profit shown on charts is operating profit (EBITDA in the xlsx). ZAR throughout.
