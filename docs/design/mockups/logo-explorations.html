homefit.studio — logo explorations

Every candidate is a valid training-plan rendering. The grammar is the one progress_pill_matrix.dart draws: coral pills, 8px gap, 3px rails, 15%-alpha circuit band, 6px band inset, rounded corners. If the progress-pill matrix couldn't produce it for a real plan, it isn't in this document.

1. The Bookend

1 standalone · 2-ex circuit × 2 rounds · 1 standalone

Carl's original idea. The two bookend pills frame a circuit block and give the mark a readable "start → work → finish" arc. Symmetric, 4 columns wide. Risk: gets narrow at small sizes — the circuit grid is only 2×2.

2. The Block

one 2-ex circuit × 3 rounds

A 2-wide, 3-tall grid inside a single circuit band. No standalones competing for attention. Reads as a brand tile. The band fully encloses the mark, so the coral tint does structural work, not decoration.

3. The Cube

one 3-ex circuit × 3 rounds

The most iconic shape — a perfect 3×3 grid. Square silhouette holds up at favicon size. No rail stubs (no standalones). The coral band + 9 pills is the cleanest expression of "a plan is a grid".

4. The Pyramid

1 standalone · 3-ex circuit × 2 rounds · 1 standalone

Wide middle, narrow ends. The bookends flank a 3×2 circuit core. Horizontal emphasis — works well in a wordmark lockup beside "homefit.studio". 5 columns wide, so it's the most demanding at tiny sizes.

5. The Stacked

1 standalone · 2-ex circuit × 4 rounds · 1 standalone

Tall circuit band between two bookends. Vertical emphasis — feels like a tower, or repetition. The 4-round height gives the coral band real body, so the brand colour gets more surface area than in the Bookend.

6. The Chain

one 3-ex circuit × 2 rounds

A 3-wide, 2-tall grid. Landscape orientation, horizontal body. Six pills in one band — the simplest "work in progress" read. Good as a flat app icon; less good as a favicon because it's wider than it is tall.

7. The Full Session

2-ex circuit × 2 rounds · 1 standalone · rest

Carl's full-sequence idea: 1-2, 1-2, then one, then rest. Reads left-to-right as an actual training plan — circuit body, a finisher, and a breath. Sage rest pill closes the sequence with the "a break is coming" signal.
