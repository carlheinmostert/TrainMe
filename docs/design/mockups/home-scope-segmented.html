Home scope segmented control

Two top-level scopes — Clients (today) and Classes (future) — live as a permanent segmented control just below the identity row. Today the Classes segment routes to a locked teaser body advertising the multi-client content model. When Classes ships, the only thing that changes is the body that the second segment renders — the control, the identity chrome, and the corner icons all stay put. No IA reshuffle on launch day.

What sells the future

  • The control itself is the advert — Classes is permanently visible from the moment the practitioner opens the app.
  • The teaser body shows real-looking class cards behind a lock — feels like a product preview, not a placeholder.
  • The Subscription / One-time mode pills tell the commercial story without copy.

What doesn't change later

  • Identity row, brand lockup, corner icons, and the segmented-control geometry are pixel-identical between today and launch day.
  • The "Soon" pill disappears; the segment label and tap target stay where they were.
  • The body callsite becomes a real ClassesListScreen — same scrolling area, same spacing.

03Why not the alternatives

Four IA primitives considered. Segmented control wins on advertising-power per pixel and on zero-change-on-ship-day.

Pattern Strength Why not
Bottom tab bar iOS-native, persistent, scales to 3-5 Steals 56px of vertical real estate forever; over-promises a third scope we haven't designed.
Segmented control (chosen) Single pixel-perfect IA primitive; both scopes always visible —
Horizontal PageView swipe Gestural; reuses the existing Capture↔Studio idiom Classes is a swipe away — weaker as an advertisement; dot indicator carries little semantic weight.
Mode chip in header Most compact Future surface hidden behind a tap — fails the "advertise it" test.
Unified Library with sections Honest about the underlying data model Forces vertical scroll past Classes content the user can't yet use; section headers don't scale to a third domain.
