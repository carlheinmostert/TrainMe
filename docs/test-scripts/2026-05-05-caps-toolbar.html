2026-05-05 · CAPS toolbar

Device QA — CAPS toolbar redesign

Scope: the Studio bottom workflow pill becomes the CAPS pill — Capture · Adjust · Preview · Publish · Share. Pill geometry goes fully-rounded (was 12px square). Each cell shows a glyph in a small dark circle plus a 10pt uppercase label below. Slim coral arrows replace the chunky triangles. Share switches to the three-dot network glyph (Icons.share).

Why: "CAPS" is a memorable mnemonic for the five workflow stages. Labels under each glyph eliminate the icon-only ambiguity. Geometry alignment with the help page ToolbarStrip.

What did NOT change: callback wiring (renamed identifiers only), workflow gate semantics (dim cell → dim incoming arrow), publish state machine (cloud-upload / spinner / error), locked-state coral lock pill replacing Publish slot. No schema, no SQLite bump, no SharedPrefs key.

Prerequisites

  • PR for branch feat/caps-toolbar-redesign merged + ./install-device.sh from repo root.
  • One client with at least one published session and one client with an empty session for the dimmed-state checks.
  • Optional: one client with a session past its 14-day grace (locked) for the locked-state check.

1 · Visual identity (mobile)

  • 1. Open a session in Studio. The bottom toolbar shows a fully-rounded pill (no square corners visible). Pill has a subtle drop shadow that lifts it off the surface.
  • 2. Pill has 5 cells. Each cell shows a glyph inside a small dark circle (~36pt) with a 10pt uppercase label below: "CAPTURE", "ADJUST", "PREVIEW", "PUBLISH", "SHARE". Labels read horizontally, not stacked weird.
  • 3. Slim coral arrows sit between the glyph circles (not between the labels). Each arrow looks lighter / less chunky than the prior triangles. Arrows feeding INTO a dim cell are also dim.
  • 4. Glyphs are visibly smaller (~20pt) than the previous toolbar's 28pt — but the cell footprint is bigger because the label sits below. Overall the pill is a similar height to before but reads as labelled buttons rather than bare icons.

2 · Active vs dim behaviour

  • 5. Open a brand-new session with no exercises captured yet (or use New Session FAB on a client). CAPTURE is bright. ADJUST, PREVIEW, PUBLISH, SHARE are all dim — the glyph dim AND the label dim (~55% mute).
  • 6. Capture one exercise, swipe back to Studio. ADJUST + PREVIEW light up (both glyph and label). PUBLISH + SHARE remain dim.
  • 7. Publish the plan. SHARE lights up. PUBLISH stays bright (re-publish state).
  • 8. If you have a locked plan past its 14-day grace (or you can simulate by opening one), the PUBLISH slot becomes a coral lock cell with the label "UNLOCK". Tapping it opens the unlock bottom sheet (existing behaviour preserved).

3 · Functional taps

  • 9. Tap CAPTURE. The session shell PageView swipes to camera mode (same as the right-edge swipe-left pull tab).
  • 10. Tap ADJUST on a session with at least one exercise. The full-screen editor sheet opens onto the topmost (most recent) non-rest exercise — same target as the prior Refine button.
  • 11. Tap PREVIEW. The unified preview screen opens (same workout preview as before).
  • 12. Tap PUBLISH on a publishable session. The publish flow fires (spinner appears in the cell during publish, "Published ✓" toast on success, error glyph + tap-for-details on failure).
  • 13. Tap SHARE on a published plan. The iOS share sheet opens with the plan URL ready. Bonus: the SHARE cell glyph is the three-dot network icon (three small circles connected by diagonal lines), not the prior arrow-out-of-box.

4 · Help page parity (web)

  • 14. Open https://manage.homefit.studio/getting-started (or the Vercel preview URL on this PR). The 5 stages read "Capture · Adjust · Preview · Publish · Share" everywhere on the page — intro callout, ToolbarStrip table-of-contents, step section titles ("Capture", "Adjust (Studio)"), body copy. No leftover "Camera" or "Refine" in user-facing prose (component names like RefineSubsection are internal — ignore those).
  • 15. Visual ToolbarStrip on the help page still renders correctly — 5 icons inside a fully-rounded pill, with "Capture · Adjust · Preview · Publish · Share" labels below. No regression in the web-portal layout.
