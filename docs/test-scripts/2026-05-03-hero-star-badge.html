Device QA — Hero star badge

Scope: small additive change. Every Studio-side mini-preview that shows an exercise thumbnail now renders a small coral star badge in the top-left corner of the preview area, indicating “this is the Hero frame”. The star is always visible for video exercises (auto-picked motion-peak counts as the active Hero until the practitioner overrides via the trim panel).

Spec recap: filled Icons.star_rounded in coral #FF6B35, 14×14 logical px, 6px from top + 6px from left. No plate / backdrop — just the glyph with a subtle 1px black drop-shadow for legibility on light thumbnails. Video ALWAYS, photo NEVER, rest NEVER. Long-press 240px peek strips chrome (and the badge along with it).

Surfaces affected: MiniPreview (Studio exercise cards, exercise-editor sheet header) + CaptureThumbnail (Home recent-clients, ClientSessions session cards, Camera viewfinder peek box, Thumbnail peek closed state).

Out of scope: web player, portal, trim-panel star (a separate parallel change). No schema or RPC edits.

Prerequisites

  • Branch worktree-agent-acfa6ea5a44998c42 built and installed via ./install-device.sh (or simulator via ./install-sim.sh).
  • At least one client with a session that has a video exercise, a photo exercise, and a rest period — needed to verify the show / hide logic in one pass.
  • (Optional) A second video exercise where you've never opened the trim panel — confirms the badge shows for auto-picked Hero frames as well as practitioner-picked ones.

1 · Studio mini-preview

  • 1. Video card — coral star top-left. Open Studio on a session with a video exercise. The exercise card's mini-preview shows a small coral filled star at the top-left corner of the preview (~6px from top, ~6px from left). Star is the glyph only — no white plate, no circular backdrop. Star sits in front of the video frame.
  • 2. Photo card — NO star. The same session's photo exercise card shows NO star. Photo is the Hero by definition; a badge would be redundant.
  • 3. Rest period — NO star. A rest row in Studio shows NO star anywhere. Rest periods have no media, so there's nothing to badge.
  • 4. Auto-picked Hero counts. A video exercise where you've never tapped “Set as Hero” in the trim panel still shows the star. The auto-picked motion-peak frame is the active Hero — the badge confirms it.
  • 5. Drop-shadow legibility. If the exercise has a light or pale Hero frame (e.g. white wall, bright skin), the coral star remains clearly visible against the background — the 1px black drop-shadow under the glyph is doing its job. No washed-out / invisible state.

2 · Camera + thumbnail peek

  • 6. Camera viewfinder peek — star on video. Capture flow: open Camera mode on a session that has at least one video. The bottom-left peek box (last-captured thumbnail) shows the coral star top-left when the most recent capture is a video.
  • 7. Camera peek — NO star on photo. Capture a photo. The peek box updates to the new photo thumbnail and the star disappears (photo is the Hero by definition).
  • 8. ThumbnailPeek closed state. In Studio, the ThumbnailPeek row (small thumbnail strip / closed peek) shows the same coral star top-left on each video tile and no star on photo / rest tiles.
  • 9. Long-press 240px peek — NO star. Long-press an exercise to trigger the large 240px preview. The big peek view drops chrome (no media-type badge, no star). Confirms the showChrome gate is honoured so nothing “pops” mid-animation.

3 · Cross-screen consistency

  • 10. Home / ClientSessions cards. Anywhere a session card shows an exercise thumbnail (Home recent activity, per-client session list), the rule still holds — coral star top-left on video tiles only. Same size + position as Studio cards.
  • 11. Exercise editor sheet header. Tap a video exercise to open the editor sheet. The header's MiniPreview still shows the star top-left (since chrome stays on for the editor's resting state).
  • 12. Tap-through unaffected. Tapping the video card / thumbnail still opens the editor sheet / media viewer as expected. The star sits in an IgnorePointer — gestures fall through to the host card.
