A · Capture & data path

  • 1. Fresh capture persists sets=3 / reps=10 / breather=15 to the cloud.
    1. Capture a fresh exercise on a fully-consented client. Don't touch reps / sets / breather.
    2. Open the Studio card → PACING accordion. Expect SETS=3, REPS=10, POST REP BREATHER=15s all pre-populated.
    3. Publish.
    4. Open the plan URL in Safari. Expect grammar reads "3 sets · 10 reps" in the active-slide title.
    5. Bonus: if you have supabase db query --linked handy, confirm SELECT sets, reps, inter_set_rest_seconds FROM exercises WHERE plan_id='<plan>' returns 3, 10, 15 (not nulls).

B · Web-player chrome

  • 2. Vertical chrome stack on the right edge — no ambient dim.
    1. Open any published plan in Safari.
    2. Expect: fullscreen toggle at top-right (top:12, right:12), settings gear directly BELOW it (top:60, right:12), mute pill to the LEFT of fullscreen (top:12, right:60). Three buttons forming an L-shape in the top-right.
    3. Tap fullscreen → enter ambient mode. Expect the chrome buttons stay at full opacity (no dim to 30%).
    4. Tap the gear → popover anchors below it (top:108, right:12) without overlapping the gear.
  • 3. Footer carries a discreet build marker that reads the current cache name.
    1. Scroll to the footer of any plan URL.
    2. Below the "powered by homefit.studio" wordmark, expect a tiny mono-font marker reading v51-photos-three-treatment (or higher if newer pushes have happened).
    3. Style: 9px mono, 55% opacity, dim grey. Should be visible but not loud.

C · "Show me" override (gear popover)

  • 4. Gear popover surfaces "Show me" segmented control + Enhanced background switch.
    1. Tap the gear button. Popover opens.
    2. Expect at the top: heading "Show me", then a 4-position segmented control: Practitioner's choice · Drawn · B&W · Colour.
    3. Below the divider: Enhanced background switch with hint text "Body in focus — background dimmed".
    4. Default: Practitioner's choice active; Enhanced background switch ON.
  • 5. Override flips the treatment globally for every video on the plan.
    1. From "Practitioner's choice", tap Drawn. Expect: every video slide on the plan now plays the line-drawing.
    2. Tap B&W. Expect: every video plays the grayscale (segmented) variant.
    3. Tap Colour. Expect: every video plays the original (segmented) variant.
    4. Tap back to Practitioner's choice. Expect: each slide reverts to its per-exercise preferred_treatment.
  • 6. Override persists per-plan in localStorage.
    1. Set "Show me" to Colour on plan A. Close the tab.
    2. Reopen plan A. Expect: still on Colour.
    3. Open a DIFFERENT plan B (any other client). Expect: starts at Practitioner's choice (per-plan key, not global).
    4. Self-heal: if you previously set Colour on a plan and the practitioner has since revoked Colour consent, the override silently resets to Practitioner's choice on next open.
  • 7. Unconsented options are disabled (visible) with a lock glyph.
    1. Open a plan for a client who has consented to line_drawing only (e.g. caitlyns or Kosie).
    2. Tap the gear. Expect: B&W and Colour segments visible but greyed out with a small lock glyph.
    3. Tap a locked segment. Expect: nothing happens (no error, no override).
  • 8. Enhanced background switch disables when treatment = Drawn (no background to dim).
    1. Set "Show me" to Drawn.
    2. Expect: Enhanced background switch greys out; hint text reads "Background dimming applies to colour playback only".
    3. Switch back to Colour. Expect: switch returns to enabled, retaining its last on/off state.

D · Vertical rep-block stack

  • 9. Stack visibility — show on multi-rep slides, hide on trivial / rest / legacy.
    1. Multi-rep slide (sets≥1, reps≥2): vertical column visible on the LEFT edge of the video.
    2. Photo slide: also visible (photos count reps too).
    3. sets=1 AND reps=1 slide: hidden (nothing meaningful to count).
    4. Rest slide: hidden (rest has its own countdown overlay).
    5. Legacy slide where reps=null in the data: hidden.
  • 10. Bottom-up stacking with section labels and connector brackets.
    1. For a sets=3 / reps=10 / breather=15 slide, the column has from BOTTOM to TOP: [10 coral rep blocks (Set 1)][1 sage rest][10 coral (Set 2)][1 sage rest][10 coral (Set 3)][1 sage rest].
    2. Section labels to the LEFT of the column: S1, R, S2, R, S3, R (top to bottom, since column is bottom-up).
    3. Coral text for set labels, sage text for rest labels. Thin connector brackets visually connect each label to its section.
  • 11. Uniform block sizing — rest matches a single rep block.
    1. Look at a sets=1 / reps=10 / breather=15 slide. Stack has 11 blocks total (10 rep + 1 trailing rest).
    2. Expect: ALL 11 blocks are the same physical height. The sage rest block isn't shorter / thinner / different-shaped — colour alone signals category.
  • 12. Active rep block doubles as a per-rep progress bar.
    1. Start a workout on a multi-rep slide.
    2. Expect: the next-to-land block has a 1Hz coral pulse on its outline.
    3. Expect: the active block's coral fill flows smoothly from 0% to 100% over its rep window (e.g. 5s for a 50s/10rep set).
    4. When the rep "lands", the block becomes solid coral; the next block becomes active starting fresh at 0% fill.
  • 13. Trailing rest after the last set runs its full duration before slide advances.
    1. Complete the last set of a slide (all 10 reps land; final block fills solid).
    2. Expect: the trailing sage rest block above starts filling, runs the full breather seconds (15s default).
    3. Slide advances to the next slide ONLY after the trailing rest finishes.
    4. During the trailing rest, the breather countdown chip overlays the video (sage, with the resting-person glyph).
  • 14. Drain animation on slide change — old fills shrink to 0 over 350ms before rebuild.
    1. Let one slide complete (stack fully filled).
    2. Watch the slide-change moment. Expect: the filled rep + rest blocks visibly drain to 0 over ~350ms before the new slide's empty stack rebuilds.
    3. No instant snap-to-empty.
    4. No 2-second "stale full" lag (which was the v49 fix — verify it's not back).
  • 15. Circuit slides treat each member-round as 1 set, regardless of slide.sets.
    1. Open a 2-exercise circuit with 3 rounds. Each circuit slide on the player has slide.sets=3 in the data.
    2. Expect: each circuit slide's stack has ONLY 1 set group (10 rep blocks + 1 trailing rest), NOT 3 set groups.
    3. The matrix at the top shows the circuit row with N pills (one per circuit member × round). The active pill spans one slide at a time.
  • 16. Rep stack reaches the top exactly when the set phase ends (no time-vs-rep drift).
    1. For a slide with custom_duration_seconds set short (e.g. 10s for 10 reps), the stack's last block becomes solid filled at the exact moment the set phase transitions to the trailing rest.
    2. No "stack reaches 8 then jumps to 10" gap.
    3. No "stack does 2 cycles per slide" cycling.
    4. For a slide where the video loop is longer than per-set/reps (e.g. 4s video × 10 reps = 40s actual but per-set says 30s), the stack still pacing-tracks the timer (duration is the source of truth), not the loop count.

E · Video playback

  • 17. Dual-video crossfade hides the loop seam.
    1. Watch a short video (4-5s) loop several times during a set.
    2. Expect: no visible stutter / jolt at the loop seam. The transition from end-frame back to start-frame appears continuous (200ms opacity crossfade between two stacked <video> elements).
    3. If you open Web Inspector → Elements, expect .video-loop-pair > .video-loop-slot with two stacked <video> tags (one data-active="true", one data-active="false").

F · Soft trim editor (Wave 20 — Flutter)

  • 18. Trim panel surfaces in _MediaViewer for video exercises only.
    1. In Studio, tap a video exercise's thumbnail → _MediaViewer opens (per-exercise full-screen preview).
    2. Expect: a trim panel pinned to the bottom (above safe-area), ~96px tall, full-width minus 12px gutter.
    3. Two coral drag-handle pills (left + right) on a horizontal scrubber. Coral fill between them; greyed bookends outside.
    4. Live readout: Trimmed: m:ss · N reps loops in m:ss.
    5. Photo cards: trim panel hidden entirely; bottom chrome (mute pill, page dots, play/pause overlay) returns to original lower position.
    6. Rest rows: trim panel hidden (no peek surfaces at all).
  • 19. Drag handles persist trim instantly; no save button.
    1. Drag the right handle inward by ~30%. Expect: the playing video's loop tightens to the new window in real time.
    2. Cannot drag handles past each other — minimum 0.3s window. Bumping the guard fires a haptic pulse.
    3. Tap inside the coral range: scrubs the video to that position. Tap in the greyed bookends: no-op.
    4. Close the viewer + reopen the same exercise. Expect: handles render at the saved positions; "⤺ Reset to full video" link visible.
    5. Tap Reset. Expect: handles snap to extremes; trim cleared.
  • 20. Web player respects trim on published plans.
    1. Trim a video exercise in _MediaViewer (e.g. 1.5s in, 7.0s out).
    2. Publish the plan.
    3. Open the plan URL in Safari. Expect: video starts at the trim in-point and loops within the trim window only.
    4. Switch treatments via "Show me" — same trim window applies across all three treatments (line / B&W / colour share source timing).
    5. Legacy plans (no trim metadata): plays the full clip, no clamp.
