2026-04-22 · Web-player top-stack v1

Device QA — Web-player top-stack

Purpose: verify the wireframe-signed-off restructure of the client web player. Top stack above the video now reads top-to-bottom: plan-title + client, 3-number ETA row (full width centered), progress-pill matrix, active-slide name + decoded grammar. Video is the hero with all chrome overlaid: mid-height chevrons (left/right), mute + NEW fullscreen toggle (top-right), notes as plain coral text at the bottom (no box; 3-line clamp — tap to expand), prep + pause overlays centered. Rest slides get a new big-coral-number countdown overlaid on the last visible frame. Behavioural additions: prep play-gating (video paused at frame 0 during runway), fullscreen ambient mode (top stack hides, coral overlays drop to ~30% alpha, tap reveals for 3s), Start Workout triggers fullscreen, workout-complete has a celebratory coral glow + scale-in check.

Retired entirely: nav-dots (bottom row of dots), peek-panel (centered teaching peek + long-press preview), treatment-seg (client-facing segmented control — practitioner's per-exercise preferred_treatment is authority), card-position ("Exercise 3"), bottom nav-bar, swipe-hint, card-level name/body/notes-box.

In scope: web-player bundle (web-player/index.html + styles.css + app.js); mobile-portrait-only. Propagates to the Flutter practitioner preview via the WKURLSchemeHandler (R-10 parity is architecturally enforced by the unified-player bundle).

Out of scope: Flutter code changes. Backend / Supabase. Middleware. Portal. Audio / treatment logic (unchanged). Desktop / landscape layouts.

Prerequisites

  • feat/web-player-top-stack-v1 deployed to session.homefit.studio (auto-deploys on push via Vercel), OR the branch installed onto the device via ./install-device.sh so the embedded WebView picks up the new bundle.
  • Hard-reload the player URL (iOS Safari: tap-and-hold the reload icon → Request Website, or add a dummy query param) — the service worker bumps from v19-per-slide-treatment to v20-top-stack but needs one cold load.
  • A published plan with: at least one exercise that has practitioner notes, at least one rest slide, and at least one circuit (so the "· Round X of Y" naming works).

1 · Top-stack ordering above the video

  • 1. Open the plan link in Safari (NOT in the Flutter app). The chrome above the video reads, top-to-bottom, as four stacked rows: (a) plan title + client name on the left, slide-progress text (e.g. 1 of 7) on the right; (b) the 3-number ETA row full-width centered (current · total · ~finish); (c) the progress-pill matrix (horizontally scrolling); (d) the exercise name in Montserrat + the decoded grammar line (e.g. 3 sets · 10 reps · 5s hold) underneath. Below that is the video viewport.

2 · Retired chrome is gone

  • 2. No dot indicators at the bottom of the viewport (nav-dots retired). No "Line · B&W · Original" segmented control (treatment-seg retired — the practitioner's per-exercise preferred treatment drives the video). No "Exercise N" label above the exercise name (card-position retired). No "Swipe to navigate" hint on first card. No bottom nav-bar bar with prev/next text buttons.

3 · Edge chevrons overlay the video mid-height

  • 3. Subtle coral chevrons sit overlaid on the left and right edges of the video at roughly mid-height. On the first slide, the left chevron is disabled (dimmed ~15%) and non-tappable. On the last slide, the right chevron is disabled. Tapping an enabled chevron advances to the next slide. Swiping the card still works too.

4 · Mute + fullscreen toggles sit top-right

  • 4. On an exercise slide with include_audio, two small coral icon buttons sit at the top-right corner of the video: mute toggle on the left, fullscreen toggle on the right (~12pt from the edge). Tapping mute flips the speaker icon + mutes every live video. Tapping fullscreen enters browser fullscreen — the icon swaps to a "collapse" glyph and the top stack + footer hide. Tap the icon again to exit; icons and top stack restore.

5 · Notes overlay as plain coral text at the bottom of the video

  • 5. On an exercise that has practitioner notes, the note reads as plain coral italic text at the bottom of the video — no box, no background pill. Text clamps to 3 lines max with an ellipsis on the third line for long notes. Tapping the note expands it to full height. Tapping again re-collapses. On an exercise with no notes, nothing is visible there. Rest slides show no note either.

6 · Circuit round context appends to the name

  • 6. Navigate to a circuit exercise (unrolled — round 2 or 3, not round 1). The active-slide header reads, for example: Goblet squat · Round 2 of 3. The decoded grammar line below stays correct for that round (10 reps · 5s hold, no "sets" on circuits). Round 1 also shows · Round 1 of 3 for consistency.

7 · Prep play-gating — video paused during the runway

  • 7. Tap Start Workout. The prep overlay appears with big coral countdown digits (5s default, or the per-exercise prep_seconds). During the runway, the video behind the digits is paused on the first frame — nothing is moving. Digits tick down 5 → 4 → 3 → 2 → 1. When the runway ends, the overlay clears AND the video starts playing.

8 · Rest countdown overlay — big coral number

  • 8. Advance to a rest slide during workout mode (or skip forward via the right chevron). A large coral digit (same visual language as the prep overlay) is overlaid on the rest card, counting down each second: 30, 29, 28… The decoded grammar row above still reads 30s rest. When the rest hits zero, the overlay hides and the player advances to the next exercise.

9 · Fullscreen ambient mode — chrome fades, reveal on tap

  • 9. Enter fullscreen via the top-right toggle. The top stack (plan title, ETA, pills, active-slide header) AND the footer are hidden. Edge chevrons + mute + notes are present but drop to ~30% alpha, so the video reads as the primary surface. Tap the video once — overlays pop to 100% alpha for 3 seconds, then fade back. The fullscreen toggle icon itself stays at 100% alpha the whole time (so the exit affordance is always obvious).

10 · Start Workout CTA centered + triggers fullscreen

  • 10. On plan load, the Start Workout button is centered over the first slide's video (not tucked at the bottom). Tapping it: (a) hides the button, (b) requests browser fullscreen — the top stack disappears (because Start is inside the user-gesture), (c) kicks off the 5s prep countdown with the big coral digits + paused video behind.

11 · Workout Complete — celebratory cue

  • 11. Run through a short plan (or skip to the last slide via the right chevron + let its timer expire). The Workout Complete overlay appears with: (a) a coral radial glow behind the checkmark (fades in over ~2s, reaches peak ~40% through), (b) the checkmark icon scales in from 60% → 100% over 200ms (not a snap — a clear pop), (c) the "Workout Complete!" title + total time + Close button below. Tap Close: overlay hides, fullscreen exits (back to browse mode), Start Workout button re-appears.

12 · ETA drifts forward while paused

  • 12. During workout mode on an exercise slide, tap the video surface to pause the timer. The pause disc appears centered. Observe the 3-number ETA row: the two coral+white numbers (current + total) freeze, but the muted grey "~finish" wall-clock number drifts forward by one minute every 60 seconds. Tap again to resume — pause disc hides, timers tick normally.
