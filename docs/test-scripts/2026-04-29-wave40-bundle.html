A · Mobile camera + Studio toolbar refresh

  • 1. M1 · Studio toolbar — Camera replaces Library as slot 1.
    1. Open any session in Studio. Look at the bottom toolbar's left-of-centre cluster.
    2. Slot 1 is a coral video-camera glyph (filled-camera-with-lens shape). It replaces the photo-library stack icon that used to live there.
    3. Tap the camera icon. The shell pages right to Capture mode (same animation as swiping right on the Studio canvas). No bottom-sheet, no modal.
    4. Swipe back to Studio. Confirm the workflow chain reads Back · Camera → Preview → Publish/Unlock → Share with coral triangles between each step. No second slot for "Library".
  • 2. M2 · Library import inside Camera — bottom-left round button opens iOS multi-select picker.
    1. In Capture mode, look at the bottom-left of the viewfinder (~16pt from screen edges). There's a 44×44pt round translucent-black button with a photo-library glyph (rectangle + circle + mountain).
    2. It sits well below the peek box (which stays mid-left, mid-height). The two affordances don't overlap.
    3. Tap the library button. The iOS multi-select photo picker opens (Photos → multi-select sheet, NOT a single-pick page).
    4. Pick 2 or 3 photos. Confirm.
    5. Each picked photo fires the SAME peek-box animation that a fresh capture does — one fly-in animation per photo, with a small stagger so they're visually distinct. The capture count badge increments by N.
    6. Swipe to Studio. The N imported photos land as new exercise cards in the order they were picked. Each card initially shows a converting spinner; spinners settle to green checks within a few seconds (Wave 39.4 photo-refresh fix).
  • 3. M3 · Permanent shutter hint — three states.
    1. Open Capture mode on a fresh client (or reset the simulator's camera-hint flag if needed). Look at the caption directly under the shutter button.
    2. Idle: reads Tap for photo · Hold for video · Slide ↑ to lock at 55% opacity, 10.5pt Inter. The caption does NOT auto-fade after 3s — it remains visible indefinitely.
    3. Press and hold the shutter (without releasing). Caption swaps to Slide finger ↑ onto 🔒 to lock. Still 55% opacity.
    4. Drag finger up onto the lock target (see item 4); release to snap locked. Caption swaps to Tap ⬛ to stop.
    5. Stop the recording. Caption returns to the idle copy.
  • 4. M4 · Slide-up-to-lock recording gesture.
    1. In Capture mode, press and hold the shutter to start a video recording. Confirm the heavy-impact haptic + the existing per-second tick haptics fire (Wave 8 behaviour preserved).
    2. Within ~200ms of recording start, a 56pt round lock target fades in directly above the shutter (~80pt centre-to-centre gap). It has a translucent-black background, white border, and a lock-outline glyph.
    3. Between shutter and lock target, a thin 4pt-wide vertical drag-track gradient is visible.
    4. While keeping the finger pressed, slide upward onto the lock target. The lock target's background tints coral as your finger crosses the threshold.
    5. Release the finger ON the lock target. A heavy-impact haptic fires. The lock target turns fully coral. The shutter inner morphs from a red rounded-square to a small WHITE square. Recording continues without your finger pressed.
    6. Per-second tick haptics keep firing — recording is genuinely live in hands-free mode.
    7. Tap the white-square shutter inner. Recording stops cleanly — light-impact haptic fires, the new exercise card lands in Studio, peek box animates.
  • 5. M4 fallback · release without locking still stops recording (no regressions).
    1. Press and hold the shutter to start recording. Do NOT drag up.
    2. Release the finger straight down. Recording stops as it always did — light haptic, card appears in Studio.
    3. Repeat: press and hold, drag finger up halfway toward the lock target (NOT all the way), then release while still below the threshold. Recording stops — the partial drag does not snap-lock.
    4. Repeat: press and hold, drag onto lock target so it tints coral, then drag back down past the threshold and release. Recording stops normally — the lock target releases its highlight when the finger leaves the corridor.
  • 6. M5 · Right-edge vertical lens pills.
    1. In Capture mode (idle, not recording), look at the right edge of the viewfinder, vertically centred. The lens pills .5 / 1× / 2 / 3 stack as a vertical column. 44×44pt round pills with 8pt gaps between.
    2. The active pill (default 1×) is inverted — white background, black text. Inactive pills are translucent black with a hairline white border, white text.
    3. Tap 2. The viewfinder zooms to 2x and the active highlight shifts to the 2 pill.
    4. Pinch-zoom on the viewfinder. Zoom level ramps continuously; the active pill highlight tracks within ~15% of each pill's value.
    5. Start a recording. The lens pills hide while recording is in flight (mid-clip lens switch causes visible jumps). Stop recording — pills reappear.
    6. Confirm the lens pills do NOT overlap the lock-drag corridor (centre-vertical of the screen). The slide-up gesture in item 4 should not bump into them.
  • 7. M6 · Toolbar glyph swap + sizing bump.
    1. In Studio, study the bottom toolbar. Compare against the Wave 39.4 build (or earlier screenshots) if available.
    2. Each icon container is now 48×48pt (up from 44). The icons themselves are 28pt (up from 24). The toolbar reads with more presence; thumb-zone parity with the camera shutter affordance.
    3. Glyphs are correct: Back arrow (left edge, white) · Camera-with-lens (coral, slot 1) · Play-triangle (Preview, white when active) · Cloud-upload-arrow OR Lock-outline (Publish vs Unlock state) · Share-network (3 connected dots, slot 4).
    4. Coral triangles between toolbar slots are correspondingly larger (10pt wide × 14pt tall, up from 8×12). Dim-state triangles (workflow gate not yet reached) read at the same proportionally-bumped size.
    5. Tap each toolbar icon. The selection-click haptic fires; routing works: Back → pop, Camera → Capture mode, Preview → preview screen, Publish → publish flow, Share → iOS share sheet.
