2026-05-05 · editor sheet polish

Device QA — Editor sheet floor 0.75 + frosted-scrim backdrop

Scope: two cosmetic tweaks to the per-exercise editor sheet (opens when you tap a Studio exercise card or the Refine toolbar button). (1) Floor detent raised 0.55 → 0.75 so non-Preview tabs cover ~75% of the screen on first open instead of ~55%. (2) The default 54% black modal barrier is replaced with a real frosted-scrim layer (BackdropFilter blur sigma 8 + 65% black dim) so the strip of Studio that IS visible above the sheet visibly recedes — same pattern Apple uses for Control Center / share sheets.

Why: at 0.55 the eye gets pulled to the underlying Studio screen; sharper visual hierarchy keeps focus on the editor.

What did NOT change: the Preview tab still snaps to 0.95 (full canvas), max detent (0.95), drag-up to grow, drag-down floor-snap behaviour, tab switching, dismiss gestures (tap-outside + fast fling), or any of the editor's tab content.

Prerequisites

  • PR for branch polish/editor-sheet-detent-scrim merged + ./install-device.sh from repo root.
  • Have at least one client with at least one captured exercise (any media type) so you can open the editor.

1 · Floor detent and coverage

  • 1. Tap an exercise card in Studio. The editor sheet animates up and settles at the floor detent. Eyeball check — the sheet now covers roughly three-quarters of the screen (was about half). The strip of Studio still visible at the top is noticeably narrower than before this PR.

2 · Frosted-scrim backdrop

  • 2. Look at the strip of Studio that IS visible above the sheet. It's clearly blurred — you can make out shapes (cards, the toolbar) but text on the cards is unreadable. This is a real frosted-glass effect (BackdropFilter), not just a dimmer overlay.
  • 3. The blur layer also has a heavier dim than before — underlying Studio reads as dark grey, not just slightly tinted. Coral accent colours from cards behind the sheet are muted, not popping through.

3 · Tap-to-dismiss still works

  • 4. With the sheet at the floor, tap anywhere on the blurred strip outside the sheet. The sheet dismisses (slides down + the scrim fades). Tap-to-dismiss still matches the default modal-barrier behaviour even though we replaced the default barrier.

4 · Drag behaviour intact

  • 5. Reopen the sheet. Drag the top drag-handle UPWARD slowly all the way to the top. The sheet snaps to the upper detent (0.95) so it almost fills the screen. The blur layer doesn't visually flash, jump, or break — the scrim stays uniform throughout the drag and at the top stop.
  • 6. From 0.95 (full), drag the handle DOWNWARD slowly past the floor and let go. The sheet snaps back UP to the new floor (0.75) — it does NOT dismiss (that's the existing "floor is the floor" rule, not a regression of this PR). The blur layer stays consistent through the snap-back.

5 · Preview tab unchanged

  • 7. Open the editor and switch to the Preview tab (swipe left twice from Plan, or tap the Preview tab strip if visible). The sheet snaps to 0.95 (full canvas for the embedded media player) — the Preview detent is unchanged. The blur layer stays consistent at the top stop and the player content reads normally.

6 · No frame drops

  • 8. On a real iPhone, open + close the sheet a few times in a row. No visible frame drops or jank during the open animation, no jank when the sheet settles at the floor, no jank when dismissing. BackdropFilter is GPU-cheap on modern iPhones; the RepaintBoundary wrapping it stops the blur from re-rendering on every frame of the sheet's slide animation.
Implementation: app/lib/widgets/exercise_editor_sheet.dart — _kMinDetent 0.55 → 0.75 + the modal-barrier replaced with a Stack wrapping a Positioned.fill GestureDetector over a RepaintBoundary + BackdropFilter(ImageFilter.blur(sigmaX: 8, sigmaY: 8)) + 65% black Container. Single source of truth wraps once at showExerciseEditorSheet.
