2026-05-15 - Circuit animation (SEVENTH attempt)

Device QA - Circuit lane tracer + frame geometry

Seventh attempt at a recurring bug. PR #337 (sixth attempt, shipped this morning) was confirmed inert by Carl on his iPhone TestFlight build. If this attempt fails too, STOP and escalate - do not ship an eighth attempt without re-thinking the architecture.

Why #337 failed. The sixth-attempt fix added await Promise.all(imgs.map(img -> new Promise(resolve => img.addEventListener('load', resolve)))) inside renderCircuitLanesFor() to wait for hero images to decode before measuring the frame. That works for eager images. But the lobby hero <img>s used loading="lazy", and lazy images that have not entered the viewport NEVER START LOADING - load/error events never fire, img.complete stays false. The await chain hung forever, paintLanesAndTracer() was never called, the SVG stayed empty, no animation. Circuit A's rows in a typical lobby are below the viewport on initial paint in the WKWebView embedded preview, so the bug hit every preview open.

The seventh-attempt fix:
- Hero <img> in lobby rows: loading="lazy" -> loading="eager" (both the photo render at web-player/lobby.js:1355 and the video poster at :1371). At most ~30 small JPGs per plan; cheap on cellular.
- Drop the await chain entirely. Call paintLanesAndTracer() synchronously on entry.
- The per-circuit ResizeObserver (attached BEFORE the synchronous paint) re-fires when the now-eager images decode and resize the frame.
- New MutationObserver watches the frame's childList for row changes (treatment toggles, etc.) and triggers a recompute.
- 30-frame poll + WAAPI hardening from PR #322 stays as defence-in-depth inside paintLanesAndTracer().

R-10 parity: both web-player/lobby.js and app/assets/web-player/lobby.js updated byte-identical. sw.js comment bumped to force full cache invalidation. Sensitive zone: PR body flags this as Carl-review-before-merge.

In scope: circuit lane tracer + static frame geometry, in embedded preview and live staging surfaces. Cold-cache path. Out-of-viewport-on-first-paint scenario (the critical failure mode of #337).

Out of scope: any non-circuit lobby behaviour, prep hero treatment, PDF export modal (separate fix already in flight), filmstrip sharpness.
